# FlowState -- Content Guide

## How Daily Content Is Structured

Each of the 28 days has a unique `DayContent` entry with:

### 1. Biology Text
Real hormonal explanation in plain language. Rules:
- Name the hormone (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, etc.)
- Explain what it does in 1 sentence
- Explain what it's doing TODAY specifically
- Connect it to a feeling or symptom she might have
- No medical jargon without immediate explanation
- Tone: informative, not clinical

**Good**: "Progesterone is peaking right now. This is the hormone that rises after ovulation to prepare the uterus for pregnancy. Think of it as the body's 'nesting hormone' -- it slows things down, causes bloating, and has a literal sedative effect on the brain."

**Bad**: "Elevated serum progesterone levels during the mid-luteal phase facilitate endometrial receptivity."

### 2. Feelings Array
3-5 bullet points of what she might experience. Rules:
- Physical AND emotional
- Specific, not vague ("may feel bloated" not "may feel bad")
- Include the WHY in parentheses when helpful ("cravings are real -- her body burns 100-300 extra calories in this phase")
- Never judgmental

### 3. Actions Array
5-8 concrete suggestions across categories. Each action has:
- `text`: What to actually do (specific and actionable)
- `category`: food | date | physical_touch | words | logistics | gift
- `effort`: low | medium | high

Rules:
- At least 2-3 "low effort" options (things you can do in 5 minutes)
- At least 1 "high effort" option (for when you want to go above and beyond)
- Vary categories -- don't make them all "food"
- Phase-appropriate (don't suggest hiking on day 2)

### 4. Avoid Array
2-4 things NOT to do. Rules:
- Specific behaviors, not vague ("Don't comment on what she's eating" not "Be nice")
- Explain why briefly if not obvious
- Never condescending

### 5. Hormone Levels
Relative values 0-1 for visualization:
- estrogen
- progesterone
- testosterone
- prostaglandins
- serotonin

These drive the horizontal bar chart on the daily card.

## Phase-Specific Content Themes

### Menstruation (Days 1-5)
- **Tone**: Warm, supportive, practical
- **Biology focus**: Prostaglandins, uterine contractions, iron loss, estrogen/progesterone at floor
- **Action themes**: Comfort (heating pad, painkillers), nourishment (iron-rich foods), reduced expectations (handle chores), physical comfort without pressure

### Follicular (Days 6-13)
- **Tone**: Energetic, encouraging, adventurous
- **Biology focus**: Rising estrogen, FSH and follicle development, testosterone climbing, serotonin building
- **Action themes**: Active dates, trying new things, gym together, planning, compliments, spontaneity

### Ovulation (Days 14-16)
- **Tone**: Celebratory, present, connected
- **Biology focus**: LH surge, peak estrogen, peak testosterone, pheromones, heightened senses
- **Action themes**: Date nights, physical intimacy, meaningful conversation, photos together, matching her social energy

### Early Luteal (Days 17-21)
- **Tone**: Calm, reflective, nurturing
- **Biology focus**: Corpus luteum, rising progesterone, second estrogen peak, body temperature rising
- **Action themes**: Cooking together, deep conversations, walks, comfortable evenings, proactive household help

### Late Luteal (Days 22-28)
- **Tone**: Patient, validating, gentle
- **Biology focus**: Progesterone crash, estrogen fall, serotonin decline, PMS mechanism explained
- **Action themes**: Validate feelings (don't fix), comfort food, reduce friction, heating pads, chocolate, patience, space or presence (ask which)
