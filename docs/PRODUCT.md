# FlowState -- Product Vision

## One-liner
The first cycle tracker built for partners. Understand her body, know what to do.

## Core Insight
Every period tracking app (Flo, Clue, Natural Cycles) is built for the person who menstruates. None are built for the person who loves them. FlowState fills that gap.

## The Problem
Most men have no idea what a menstrual cycle actually is. They know "period = bad mood" and that's it. The cycle is a 28-ish day hormonal symphony that affects energy, mood, sex drive, pain tolerance, sleep, appetite, skin, and emotional processing. Every day is different. Women know this because they live it. Partners don't because nobody taught them.

## How It Works

### Two Onboarding Paths

**Path A: Manual Input (knows the date)**
1. Enter the start date of her last period
2. Enter cycle length (default 28)
3. Get daily cards immediately

**Path B: AI Observation Mode (doesn't know the date)**
1. No setup required -- just start logging what you notice
2. Log behavioral observations: "she's been tired," "she had cramps," "she's really energetic," "she cried at a commercial"
3. AI cross-references observations against hormonal patterns to estimate current phase
4. After ~1 full cycle of observations, the AI locks onto her cycle and starts predicting forward
5. Over time, transitions into a proper predictive tracker based on accumulated data

Path B is the default. Path A is for users who already know the date.

### Daily Experience

Every day you get a **Daily Card** with three layers:

**Layer 1: What's happening in her body**
Real biology explained simply. Not vague -- actual hormone names, what they do, why they matter. Example: "Progesterone is peaking. Progesterone is a hormone that rises after ovulation to prepare the uterus for pregnancy. It has a sedative effect, causes bloating, and increases appetite."

**Layer 2: What she might be feeling**
Translates biology into human experience. "Energy is low. She may want to cancel plans. Cravings are real and physiological, not willpower."

**Layer 3: What you can do (multiple options)**
Concrete actions across categories (food, date ideas, physical touch, words, logistics, gifts). Each action shows effort level. Things to avoid listed separately.

## Key Features

### Daily Cards
Open the app, get today's card. 60 seconds to read. Core loop.

### Cycle Timeline
Visual ring showing where she is, which phase, what's coming next.

### AI Coach (via Bhat API)
Context-aware relationship coaching. User describes a situation ("she's upset about something small"), AI knows the cycle context and responds with biology-informed advice. Not generic relationship advice -- advice that understands the hormonal context.

### Observation Logging
For users who don't know the exact cycle date. Log what you notice (moods, energy, physical symptoms you observe). AI maps observations to estimated cycle phase. Gets more accurate over time.

### Smart Reminders
- "Her period likely starts in 2 days. Stock up on ibuprofen and chocolate."
- "Ovulation window. Date night?"
- "PMS window starts Thursday. Keep the weekend low-key."

### Learn Mode
Short educational modules: "What actually causes cramps," "The estrogen-serotonin connection," "Why her sex drive changes." 2-3 min reads that permanently level up understanding.

### Cycle Log
When her period actually starts, tap "Period started today" and the app recalibrates. Predictions get more accurate over months.

## The Four Phases

| Phase | Days (28-day) | Key Hormones | Her Experience | Partner Mode |
|-------|--------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Menstruation | 1-5 | Low everything, high prostaglandins | Cramps, fatigue, low mood then relief | Comfort: food, painkillers, heating pad, handle chores |
| Follicular | 6-13 | Rising estrogen, FSH active | Energy climbing, mood brightening, confidence building | Adventure: active dates, gym together, new experiences |
| Ovulation | 14-16 | Peak estrogen, LH surge, peak testosterone | Peak energy, highest sex drive, most social | Date night: make it count, be present, match her energy |
| Early Luteal | 17-21 | Rising progesterone, second estrogen peak | Calmer, introspective, appetite increasing | Low-key: cook together, deep conversations, walks |
| Late Luteal | 22-28 | Crashing progesterone + estrogen | PMS, mood swings, bloating, fatigue, cravings | Patience: validate feelings, comfort food, reduce friction |

## Privacy & Consent
- Prompt users to tell their partner they're using the app
- All data stored locally on device by default
- No account required
- Optional: partner can share data from their own tracker

## Monetization
- **Free**: Full daily cards, cycle tracking, observation logging
- **Premium**: AI coach, learn mode deep-dives, expanded action library, smart reminders, multi-cycle insights
- **One-time purchase option**: $5-10 unlock everything, no subscription

## Viral Angle
The original social media post that inspired this already resonates massively. The concept of "track her cycle to be a better partner" is inherently shareable. The app makes it actionable.
